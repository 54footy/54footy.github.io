  1. Cultural differences

    American players don't have soccer embedded in their DNA (yet). This is the reason America soccer is so far behind other countries.

  2. Soccer does not lose players to other sports

    The best players in the world are those that understand the game; not the strongest or fastest. Understanding the game stems from culture and exposure.

  3. 3 rules for a beautiful game

    Any team can play beautifully by following 3 principles. 1. keep the ball on the floor, always 2. Know what you're going to do before you get the ball 3. Intelligently move into space off the ball. That's it.

  4. Travling doesn't mean better competition

    You don't need to travel 3k miles to play at the highest level. Regional competition is sufficient but requires a collective increase in quality of play that stems from culture. More travel does not mean better players.
